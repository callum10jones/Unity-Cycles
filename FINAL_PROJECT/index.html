Grab London by the Handlebars

Unity Cycles is a fun and economical way to get around town, designed with convenience in mind for quick journeys. More than 25,000 bikes are available at 1600 stations.

Get Riding

Our Service

Unity Cycle provides simple 24/7 access to 6,850 cycles and 625 stations located throughout the city's 200 square kilometres. Unity Cycle is a fun, flexible, and cost-effective way to get around London, whether you're commuting or enjoying the city with friends and family.

How It Works

Buy

A pass can be purchased at a station kiosk or through the Unity Cycles app. Riders can also sign up for an annual membership online.

Unlock

When you buy from the station kiosk or through the Unity Cycles app, you'll get a 5-digit number. To unlock and ride, enter your code into a dock with an available bike.

Ride

While your pass is active, you can take unlimited 30 or 45 minute station-to-station trips. Overage costs apply after 30 minutes. To complete your journey, return your bike to any station located across the city.

Repeat

To continue riding, select "I Have a Pass" at a station kiosk or tap a station on the map in the Unity Cycles app to request a new unlock code.

    Find a Bike

    Memberships

    Unity Cycles is a faster and more active transportation option that is perfect for getting to work or school, running errands or attending social events, and connecting to other forms of transit – saving you time and money.

    With your membership, you can take as many rides as you want while your membership is active, and the first 45 minutes of each ride are included in your plan when you ride a Unity Bike classic bike. Just sign into the Unity Bike app or pick up a key to unlock thousands of bikes across Westminister, Greenwich, Islington, Southwark and Richmond.

    Additional Pricing

    Each ride's first 45 minutes are included in the monthly membership fee. Upgrade to an e-bike for an extra 5p per minute, with a maximum of £3 for rides of 45 minutes or less that begin or stop outside Richmond. It costs 15p per minute if you keep a bike out for more than 45 minutes, regardless of kind or destination.

    Renewing Your Membership

    A new Unity Bike app was recently released, with features designed to make rides more comfortable and enjoyable. Every Reduced Fare Bike Share membership will now be a £5/month plan with automatic renewals as part of the change.

    After updating their Unity Bike app, members can enrol in the new plan via the app or the website to continue riding. It is not necessary to resubmit your eligibility paperwork.

    You can unsubscribe at any time. After RFBS members have been enrolled in the new monthly plan, they will receive a reminder email five days before their next automatic monthly membership payment, allowing them to make any necessary changes to their membership.
